Matter-antimatter asymmetries at the LHC Read lab report
Third year lab investigating the CP violation of the weak decay of the B meson. The invariant mass of the charged B meson decay to K pi pi was reconstructed and the CP violation of this channel estimated. The two-body resonances of this decay were also studied and the corresponding particles were identified using PDGLive. Dalitz plots of the decay channel were used to show local regions of CP violation and the statistical significance of these regions. Skills used in this project include: scientific programming, including non-linear curve fitting (the most complicated model used fitted 5 curves simultaneously), numpy and matplotlib; basic particle physics knowledge; statistical analysis of, for example, fitting models and uncertainties.

Monte Carlo simulation of neutron penetration Read notebook
Second year computational physics project investigating the penetration of fast neutrons into various materials using Monte Carlo techniques. The simulation simulated the transmission properties neutron shielders, such as water and lead. A statistical analysis of each simulation was performed to estimate the uncertainties on each of the rates measured: transmission, reflection, and absorption. The Woodcock tracking method was also implemented to test cases of composite materials.
